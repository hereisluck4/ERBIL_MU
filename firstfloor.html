School Museum Erbil

Ali Hammadi hall

Ali Hammadi, a sports teacher, played a major role in the school’s sports championships in the 1960s.

Hall of Khairallah Abdul Karim

Hall of Khairallah Abdul Karim. This hall is dedicated to the memory of professors and teachers who sacrificed their life/were killed in the events of the Kurdistan Freedom March in the last century.

Mustafa Shaaban hall

Mustafa Shaaban was an outstanding teacher in the 1950s and 1960s. He established dormitories for students from poor families. Some of his pupils became famous political figures later, among them Mam Jalal al Talabani the former president of Iraq (2005-2014).

Ismail Sarhang hall

Ismail Sarhang was a dedicated science teacher in the 1970s. He was passionate about growing plants and played a great role in exploring and documenting the flora of the Kurdistan Region.

Shukriya Jiawk hall

Shukriya Jiawk was the first female Kurdish teacher in Erbil. Starting her career as a teacher in Erbil’s first girl school in 1936, she played a major role in encouraging Kurdish families to send their girls to school.

Daniel Qassab hall

Daniel Qassab was an art education teacher at the school in the 1930s and 1940s. He founded the fine art movement in Erbil. He was of Jewish faith and lived in Erbil, which he had to leave in 1951.

Khader Mouloud hall

Khader Mouloud, one prominent school principal (mid-1960s to 1970s), can be called a pioneer of media-education. He established a cinema room in the school.
